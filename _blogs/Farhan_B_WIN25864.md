title: "Learning to Sit With the Struggle"
author: Farhan Iqbal

I came into this internship with a familiar mindset: finish tasks, tick boxes, move on. Somewhere along the way, that mindset quietly stopped working.

This internship didn’t let me rush past confusion. It didn’t reward surface-level completion. Instead, it placed me in situations where I had to stay — stay with a problem, stay with uncertainty, stay with my own discomfort of not knowing. That was unfamiliar, and at times, frustrating. But it was also where real learning began.

At first, learning through ViBe felt different from anything I had done before. There was no sense of being “spoon-fed.” Progress depended entirely on how honestly, I engaged. Some days I felt confident; other days I questioned whether I was actually understanding anything at all. What surprised me was that both days mattered equally. Showing up consistently, even when clarity was missing, slowly built something deeper than confidence — it built trust in my own process.

The case studies were a turning point. They didn’t ask for the right answer; they asked for my thinking. I struggled. I second-guessed myself. I realized how often I looked for validation instead of reasoning things out. Over time, I learned to pause, break problems down, and accept that struggling didn’t mean failing — it meant learning was happening.

One of the most unexpected parts of this journey was collaboration. Discussions in breakout rooms, listening to how others approached the same problem differently, and defending my own ideas taught me that learning isn’t solitary. It’s shaped through dialogue, disagreement, and shared confusion. I stopped seeing questions as a weakness and started seeing them as contributions.

What stayed with me after the sessions ended wasn’t a specific framework or syntax. It was the realization that learning can be uncomfortable and still be meaningful. That confusion doesn’t mean you’re stuck—it often means you’re moving forward.

I’m grateful to Sudarshan Iyengar (Prof. Sudarshan Iyengar), Indian Institute of Technology, Ropar, NPTEL , ANNAM.AI and Vicharanashala Lab for Education Design for creating an environment that encourages real learning, accountability, and self-improvement.
